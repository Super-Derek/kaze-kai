What Makes Us Unique

Kaze-Kai Shotokan Karate stands out as a premier martial arts studio in Connecticut. Founded by Shihan Sensei Robert Goral, a distinguished member of the United States Martial Arts Hall of Fame, our dojo blends traditional Shotokan techniques with modern self-defense principles. Sensei Goral's induction into the Hall of Fame recognizes his lifelong dedication to martial arts and exceptional teaching. We offer a supportive environment where students of all ages can grow and excel under the guidance of our esteemed instructors. Our "Ikken Hissatsu" philosophy emphasizes efficient and effective techniques, ensuring each student receives top-tier training rooted in decades of expertise.

Meet the Instructors

Shihan Sensei Goral

Founder

With over 50 years of martial arts experience, Sensei Goral is a United States Martial Arts Hall of Fame inductee. His journey began as a hand-to-hand combat instructor in the US Army. In 1972, he started studying under Master Ochia, achieving his 1st-degree black belt in 1978 and 3rd-degree by 1983. Sensei Goral's diverse training includes Shobu-Kai, Ryobu-Kai, and Shindo Jinen Ryu, culminating in the creation of Kaze-Kai Shotokan in 1998. Now a 7th-degree black belt, he continues to inspire students with his wisdom and dedication.

Gregg Morrison

Chief Instructor

Training since 1997, Gregg holds a 4th-degree black belt (Yondan). He embodies the philosophy "Once a student, always a student" and is dedicated to helping others achieve their martial arts goals. Gregg's teaching style combines traditional techniques with practical applications, ensuring students develop both skill and understanding.

Nermijad Mehmedovic

Senior Instructor

Nermijad began Shotokan training in Bosnia at age 13, earning his first black belt at 19. After a 12-year hiatus, he rediscovered his passion at Kaze-Kai. Now a 2nd-degree black belt (Nidan), he trains alongside his daughters, embodying the family spirit of our dojo. Nermijad's international background brings a unique perspective to his teaching.

Sam Quach

Senior Instructor

San started training under Sensei Goral at age 9. A 3rd-degree black belt (Sandan), he brings a unique perspective to teaching with his engineering background and fluency in five languages. San excels in both kata and kumite competitions, consistently placing first. His imaginative insight and technical expertise make him an invaluable asset to the Kaze-Kai team.
